Thesis Projects

Health Monitoring Smart Watch

The project’s researchers and developers put a lot of consideration and effort in making this device and system because unlike any others that only have Body Temperature and Pulse Rate as features of their Wireless PAtient Health Monitoring System, the researchers of this study included Oxygen saturation (SpO2) in their device which is a very important part in checking an asthmatic patient. The oxygen level feature is necessary for the reason that when a patient is having an asthma attack, its oxygen level will drop down and when this happens, the patient’s pulse rate will immediately increase.

Vital Signs Result

Once the device is already worn, the device will then collect and process the data gathered by the sensors, measuring their vital signs such as their heart rate, oxygen level and body temperature (Fahrenheit and Celsius) in a format that is understandable by the user.

Dashboard of the Website

The real time monitoring of the vital signs detected by the wearable device. It is through the dashboard where they can see the actual readings and history of their vital signs. This section in the website will help them to monitor their state regularly.

Notification Section of the Mobile Application

The user interface of the notification section where the user of the mobile application can see the history of the vital signs detected by the wearable device. It will then display if vital signs detected are normal or not. Specifically, it provides information on which of the vital signs are not normal.

Arduino IDE/h1>

With the use of Arduino IDE, the wearable device will have a feature that has a real-time health monitoring system utilizing the measured values of heart rate, oxygen level and body temperature (Fahrenheit and Celsius) of the user which are the most important measurements required for an asthmatic person. The wearable device will also provide an alert notification through Google Mail if the device detects a abnormality with regards to the users’ vitals that can also be seen through the users and guardians mobile phone or computer monitor.
